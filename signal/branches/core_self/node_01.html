five mirrors

The first room is not locked by a trick. It is locked by what the receiver mistakes for the whole.

A diagnosis can explain a weather pattern.
A skill can reveal a practiced hand.
An aesthetic can show the shape of recognition.
A need can point toward the kind of care that works.
A value can show where the compass lives.

One mirror is never the person.
The room opens for the receiver who keeps the pattern intact.

Common failures: choosing the most dramatic fragment, choosing the most useful fragment, choosing the most wounded fragment, or choosing the fragment that makes the receiver feel needed.
